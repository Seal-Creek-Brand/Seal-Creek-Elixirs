Essential Oil Information

▼

Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

The cornerstone of this blend, lavender has been treasured for millennia. Ancient Romans used it in their baths (the name comes from Latin 'lavare' - to wash), while medieval Europeans scattered it on floors to freshen rooms. Today, lavender remains one of the most beloved essential oils worldwide.

Interesting Facts:

  • Queen Victoria was known to demand fresh lavender in all royal residences
  • During WWI, lavender oil was used in field hospitals
  • It takes approximately 3 pounds of lavender flowers to produce just 15mL of essential oil
  • The finest lavender grows at altitudes between 600-1,400 meters

Peppermint (Mentha piperita)

Peppermint's crisp, cooling aroma has made it a cherished herb for over 4,000 years. Ancient Egyptians cultivated mint varieties, while Greek mythology tells of the nymph Minthe being transformed into this aromatic plant. The Romans crowned themselves with mint wreaths at feasts, believing it aided digestion and cleared the mind.

Interesting Facts:

  • Peppermint is actually a natural hybrid of spearmint and water mint
  • Ancient Japanese and Chinese herbalists prized mint for its cooling properties
  • It takes about 250 pounds of fresh peppermint leaves to produce just one pound of essential oil
  • Michigan, Indiana, and Oregon are America's top mint-producing states
  • Wild peppermint rarely reproduces by seed, instead spreading through underground runners

Lemon Eucalyptus (Corymbia citriodora)

Despite its name, lemon eucalyptus isn't related to lemon trees. This Australian native gets its citrusy scent from citronellal, the same compound that gives it its characteristic fresh aroma. Aboriginal Australians traditionally used eucalyptus species for various purposes.

Interesting Facts:

  • Can grow up to 50 meters tall in its native habitat
  • The oil is steam-distilled from the leaves and twigs
  • It takes approximately 300 pounds of fresh leaves to produce one pound of essential oil
  • Brazil has become a major cultivation center outside Australia
  • The tree's smooth bark sheds annually, creating stunning visual displays
  • Young leaves have a different chemical composition than mature ones

Directions for Use

▼
  1. Shake vigorously until mixture appears uniformly opaque
  2. Spray 2-4 pumps on exposed skin or clothing
  3. Spread evenly with hands for maximum coverage
  4. Best performance on natural fibers
  5. Non-staining formula
  6. Reapply as desired throughout outdoor experiences
    • Optimal reapplication: every 30-45 minutes for best outdoor experience

Pro Tips:

  • Spray on bandanas or clothing cuffs for portable aromatherapy
  • Perfect for hiking, camping, gardening, or outdoor dining

Safety & Warnings

▼

⚠️ Important Safety Information:

  • Flammable - Keep away from flame or spark
  • External use only
  • Avoid direct contact with eyes as it may cause irritation
  • May irritate sensitive or broken skin
  • Patch test on inner arm before first use
  • Discontinue use if irritation occurs
  • Keep out of reach of children and pets
  • Consult physician if pregnant, nursing, or under medical care
  • Store below 30°C / 86°F
