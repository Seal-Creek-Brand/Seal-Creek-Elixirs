Essential Oil Information

▼

Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

image

The cornerstone of this blend, lavender has been treasured for millennia. Ancient Romans used it in their baths (the name comes from Latin lavare – to wash), while medieval Europeans scattered it on floors to freshen rooms.

Interesting Facts:

  • Queen Victoria demanded fresh lavender in all royal residences
  • During WWI, lavender oil was used in field hospitals
  • ≈100 lb of flowers yield 1 lb of oil
  • Finest lavender grows at 600–1 400 m elevation

Peppermint (Mentha piperita)

image

Peppermint's crisp, cooling aroma has delighted humanity for >4 000 years. Greeks mythologized the nymph Minthe, Romans used mint wreaths during feasts, and Egyptian medical papyri list mint varieties.

Interesting Facts:

  • Natural hybrid of spearmint + water mint
  • Ancient Chinese herbalists prized its cooling nature
  • ≈250 lb of leaves yield 1 lb of oil
  • Top US producers: MI, IN, OR
  • Wild peppermint spreads via runners, not seed

Lemon Eucalyptus (Corymbia citriodora)

image

This Australian native owes its citrusy scent to high citronellal. Despite the name, it's unrelated to lemon trees.

Interesting Facts:

  • Can reach 50 m tall in situ
  • Oil steam‑distilled from leaves & twigs
  • ≈300 lb of leaves yield 1 lb of oil
  • Brazil is a major grower outside Australia
  • Bark sheds annually, revealing fresh patterns
  • Leaf chemistry shifts with plant age

Directions for Use & Safety Warnings

▼

Directions for Use

  1. Shake vigorously until mixture appears uniformly opaque
  2. Spray 2–4 pumps on exposed skin or clothing
  3. Spread evenly with hands for maximum coverage
  4. Best performance on natural fibers
  5. Non‑staining formula
  6. Reapply as desired
    • Every 15‑45 min for sustained outdoor humanizing

Pro Tips:

  • Spray on bandanas or cuffs for portable aromatherapy
  • Ideal for hiking, camping, gardening, outdoor dining

Safety & Warnings

Important Safety Information:

  • Flammable – keep away from flames
  • External use only
  • Avoid eyes; may cause irritation
  • May irritate sensitive/broken skin
  • Patch test before first use
  • Discontinue if irritation occurs
  • Keep out of reach of children & pets
  • Consult physician if pregnant/nursing
  • Store below 30 °C / 86 °F

Classification: cosmetic body mist; no therapeutic or pesticidal claims
